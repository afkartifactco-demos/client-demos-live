Editorial standards

Fast local updates still deserve careful standards.

The publication is positioned as a useful community desk: timely enough for changing weather, clear enough for families making plans, and transparent about what is known, sourced, sponsored or corrected.

Pre-launch noteThe owner can adjust these standards to match his actual reporting process before the public launch.

Verify before publishing

  • Prefer official agencies and direct sources
  • Name the location and update time
  • Separate confirmed facts from reports still being checked
  • Avoid sensational language that exceeds the source

Weather coverage

National Weather Service watches and warnings should be linked or attributed. Readers should follow official instructions and use NOAA Weather Radio or local emergency alerts for life-safety decisions.

Closings and openings

School, business and event status should come from an authorized representative or official announcement. Time-sensitive entries should include a date and last-checked time.

Corrections

Fix material errors promptly. Add a visible correction note when the original publication could have materially misled readers.

Sponsored content

Paid placements and sponsor-supported stories must be clearly labeled. Sponsors do not control emergency coverage, corrections or independent editorial judgment.

Community submissions

Submitted photos, video and tips should be reviewed for authenticity, permission and safety before reuse.
